Executive Summary

This study explores how individuals navigate the complex intersection of digital connectivity and personal privacy within the context of modern dating. Through in-depth interviews with 45 participants, we identify the emerging "Availability Paradox" and its impact on long-term relationship health.

The Availability Paradox

Constant connectivity often reduces perceived value in early-stage dating.

Digital Tethering

The psychological pressure to remain "reachable" at all times.

Privacy Negotiation

The shift from individual privacy to "shared digital space".
